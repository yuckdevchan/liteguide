# SSD
**SSD** stands for [Solid State Drive](Solid-state-drive).

An **SSD** is a **data storage device** which stores **digital information** (almost always in [binary](binary))

**SSDs** are **non-volatile** because **data is not lost** when the **device** is **powered off**.

**Data** is stored as **electrical charges** in **non-volatile memory chips**.

## Pros
- **Faster** than [HDDs](hdd) and [optical media](optical-media) due to **no moving parts**.
- **More durable** than [HDDs](hdd) because of **no moving parts**.
- **More suitable** for [mobile devices](mobile) due to **no moving parts**.
- **Less prone to failure** than [HDDs](hdd) due to **no moving parts**.
- **Lower power consumption** than [HDDs](hdd) and [optical media](optical-media) due to **no moving parts**.

## Cons
- **Costs more** per giga[byte](byte) **than other** [HDDs](hdd) and [optical media](optical-media) **storage**.
- **More prone to failure** than [optical-media](optical-media) due to **limited write cycles**.

## How it works
An **SSD** consists of **one or more memory chips** that are made up of [transistors](transistor).

**Data** is stored by **trapping** [electrons](electron) in the **transistors**. The **presence** or **absence** of **electrons** in a **transistor** can represent **binary data** (0s and 1s).
