# GPU
[GPU](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit) stands for **Graphics Processing Unit**.

(w) The terms '[graphics card / video card](graphics-card)' and 'GPU' are often used interchangeably. Most people agree that they are [not the same thing](https://www.gpumag.com/gpu-vs-graphics-card/). This is explained later.

## Uses
**GPUs** were **originally intended** for performant **3D graphics** rendering. **3D graphics** are rendered by calculating **matrix transformations**.

**GPUs** are also **used for** [machine learning](machine-learning) and [LLMs](llm) because they **also work** by calculating **matrix transformations**.

(e) The **GPU** company [NVIDIA](NVIDIA) became the **most valuable** in the world **in 2025** because their **GPU technology** could be used to **train** and run **LLMs**.

## Ways to include a GPU in a computer
**GPU chips** can be **directly built** into, or **mounted to** the [motherboard](motherboard).

**GPUs** can **also** be **integrated** into the **same chip** as the [CPU](cpu). This is called an **integrated GPU**, as **opposed** to a **discrete GPU**.

**GPUs** can be **mounted** to their **own board** which itself can be **attached to** the **motherboard**. The **resulting** board is a **graphics card**.

[eGPUs](eGPU) are a **type of GPU** which is an **external graphics card** that is **plugged into** a computer, usually **via** a [Thunderbolt](thunderbolt) [USB-C](usb-c) **port**.
