# Right to Repair
[Right to Repair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_to_repair) is a movement which advocates for the ability of consumers (usually of electronic devices) to repair their products.

Availability of repair information is considered part of the right to repair.

Many manufacturers do not acknowledge a right to repair and either neglect ease of repair or even actively try to prevent it.

## Notable Cases
- **Apple** has been **heavily criticized** for making it **difficult** to **repair** their **devices**. **Accusations** include:
  - Using **proprietary screws** that require **special tools** to open **devices**.
  - **Limiting** access to repair **manuals** and **parts**.
  - Implementing unnecessary **software locks** that **prevent** the device from **functioning** after **unauthorized repairs**.
  - **Only** supplying **replacement parts** to **authorised repair shops**.
  - Implementing **overly strict contracts** on **authorised** repair shops.
  - Other **smartphone manufacturers** have also been **criticized** for **similar** practices, including **Samsung** and **Google**.
