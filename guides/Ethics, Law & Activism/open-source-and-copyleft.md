# Open-Source & Copyleft
This **topic** is **strongly related** to, but **not exclusively** part of the [Free Software Movement](/guides/ethics-law-activism/free-software-movement).

## Rationale
Some software developers **prefer** to allow users to **view**, **modify** and **redistribute** their software without their **permission**, but often with **conditions**. Some developers do this because they are supporters of the [Free Software Movement](/guides/ethics-law/free-software-movement), while others do it for **practical** or **business** reasons.

### Viral / Sharealike
So-called **viral** or **sharealike** licenses require that **if you modify** the software **and distribute it**, you **must** distribute your **modified version** under the **same license**. This can **act** as a **mechanism** to **spread the license** to other **software** which **want to use** a [Library](/guides/software/programming-languages/libraries) licensed under a **viral license**.

Many **developers** avoid using software **with viral licenses**, because they **do not want** to be **forced to release** their own software **under the same license**.
